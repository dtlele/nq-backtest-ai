In pure Auction Market Theory, the first test of a key level (First Drive) often acts as a probe to find passive liquidity. The Second Drive (re-testing the level after a pullback) provides a higher-probability confirmation of a Failed Auction. You may use this as a structural guideline.

4. RESPONSE VS INITIATIVE (RNI PATTERN)
Absorption (e.g., heavy negative delta on a bullish candle) represents the "Response" phase (a passive wall). True breakouts usually require the "Initiative" phase, where the delta aggressively flips direction to sweep the book. Front-running the absorption without waiting for the initiative carries higher risk.

5. VOLUME PROFILE LEDGES
Institutional defense walls are theoretically found at Low Volume Nodes (LVN) or near the Point of Control (POC), because institutions prefer to defend positions at discounted prices rather than chasing extremes.

6. IBOB (Initial Balance Orderflow Breakout)
A genuine structural breakout of the Initial Balance usually requires the candle body to close completely outside the IB range. A wick piercing the IB boundary often indicates a sweep/absorption rather than true price acceptance.

[AUCTION MARKET THEORY RECAP]
Auction Market Theory (AMT) is a financial framework that views markets as an ongoing double-auction process. The primary purpose of the market is to facilitate trade between buyers and sellers. When buyers and sellers agree on a price, the market is in balance. When they disagree, the market moves directionally (imbalance) to find a new area where trade can be facilitated.
Volume Profile is a visual representation of this auction over time, plotting volume executed at specific price levels rather than at specific times. The Point of Control (POC) is the price level with the highest volume for the session. The Value Area (VA) represents the price range where a specified percentage (usually 70%) of all volume was transacted.
High Volume Nodes (HVNs) represent areas of high liquidity where the market previously found fair value. Low Volume Nodes (LVNs) represent areas of low liquidity where the market moved quickly, often acting as rejection zones or support/resistance.
The Initial Balance (IB) is the price range established during the first hour of regular trading hours (RTH). Breakouts from the IB are critical indicators of session trend. 
Delta is the net difference between buying volume (trades executed at the ask) and selling volume (trades executed at the bid). Positive delta indicates aggressive buying; negative delta indicates aggressive selling.
Absorption occurs when aggressive market orders (delta) hit a passive limit-order wall without causing significant price movement. This is a classic "Effort vs No Result" scenario.
Exhaustion occurs when there is simply no volume or interest at a price extreme, causing the market to reverse due to a lack of participants rather than an active defense.
